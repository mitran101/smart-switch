Privacy Policy

Last updated: 25 March 2026

1. Who We Are

SwitchPilot ("we", "us", "our") is an energy switching service that helps UK households find and switch to cheaper energy tariffs. Our registered address is 128 City Road, London, EC1V 2NX. For data protection queries, contact us at team@switch-pilot.com.

2. What Data We Collect

2.1 Questionnaire Data

When you complete our energy questionnaire, we collect:

  • Full name, email address, and phone number
  • Postcode and property address
  • Current energy supplier and tariff details
  • Annual energy usage (kWh) or recent bill information
  • Meter type (standard, Economy 7, smart, prepayment)
  • Tariff preferences (fixed, variable, green energy)

2.2 Switching Data

If you instruct us to switch on your behalf, we additionally collect:

  • Bank account holder name, sort code, and account number (for direct debit setup with your new supplier)
  • Meter serial number, MPAN (electricity) and/or MPRN (gas)
  • Current meter reading

2.3 Website Data

When you visit our website, we collect:

  • Email address and postcode (signup form)
  • Standard web analytics data (cookies, IP address, browser type)

3. Why We Collect Your Data (Legal Basis)

We process your data on the following legal bases under UK GDPR:

  • Contract performance (Article 6(1)(b)) - to provide you with our energy comparison and switching service
  • Consent (Article 6(1)(a)) - where you explicitly consent to us collecting and using your banking details to set up an energy account on your behalf
  • Legitimate interests (Article 6(1)(f)) - to improve our service and communicate with you about your switch

4. How We Use Your Data

We use your personal data to:

  • Compare energy tariffs and provide you with personalised recommendations
  • Set up an energy supply account with your chosen supplier on your behalf
  • Communicate with you about the progress of your switch
  • Contact you about our services (only with your consent)

5. Who We Share Your Data With

We share your data with:

  • Your chosen energy supplier - to set up your new energy account and direct debit. Once submitted, your data is subject to that supplier's own privacy policy.
  • Jotform Inc. - our secure form provider, which processes and stores your form submissions on encrypted EU-based servers. Jotform is UK GDPR compliant.

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal data with any third parties for marketing purposes.

6. How We Store and Protect Your Data

Your data is collected via encrypted forms (256-bit SSL, RSA-2048 encryption at rest) and stored on EU-based servers. Access to your data is restricted to authorised SwitchPilot personnel only.

Banking details: Your bank account details are collected solely for the purpose of setting up a direct debit with your new energy supplier. Once the switch is confirmed and your direct debit is active, we permanently delete your banking details from our systems. We do not store banking details beyond what is necessary to complete the switch.

7. How Long We Keep Your Data

  • Banking details (sort code, account number) - deleted within 7 days of your switch being confirmed
  • Questionnaire data (name, usage, preferences) - retained for up to 12 months to support your account and any follow-up queries, then deleted
  • Email and postcode (signup) - retained until you unsubscribe or request deletion

8. Your Rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the right to:

  • Access - request a copy of the personal data we hold about you
  • Rectification - ask us to correct any inaccurate data
  • Erasure - ask us to delete your data (subject to any legal obligations)
  • Restrict processing - ask us to limit how we use your data
  • Data portability - request your data in a machine-readable format
  • Withdraw consent - withdraw consent for processing at any time (this does not affect the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal)
  • Object - object to processing based on legitimate interests

To exercise any of these rights, email us at team@switch-pilot.com. We will respond within 30 days.

9. Complaints

If you are unhappy with how we handle your data, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk or by calling 0303 123 1113.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted on this page with an updated date. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.
