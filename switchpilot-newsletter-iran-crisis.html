⚠️ Special Edition: The Middle East conflict has sent UK energy markets into turmoil. Here's what it actually means for your bills - no panic, just facts.

Hi there,

You've probably seen the headlines. The US and Israel launched strikes on Iran last Friday, and the fallout has hit energy markets hard. We wanted to cut through the noise and give you the honest picture.

What happened

The Strait of Hormuz - the narrow waterway that carries roughly 20% of the world's oil and gas - has effectively shut down. Iran warned ships not to cross, and around 150 tankers are now anchored in the Persian Gulf.

On top of that, Qatar - one of the world's biggest LNG exporters - halted production at its two main facilities after Iranian drone attacks, and officially declared force majeure on March 4th. That's a legal clause that lets them cancel gas deliveries without penalty due to war.

The result? UK wholesale gas prices surged 93% in a single week. European gas prices nearly doubled.

What it means for your bills

The good news: your bills are protected until at least July. The April-June price cap has already been set at £1,641/year - actually a 7% drop from the current quarter. That's locked in regardless of what happens in the Middle East.

The concern: Cornwall Insight - the analysts Ofgem listens to most closely - are now forecasting the July-September cap at £1,801. That's a £160 increase, wiping out the April savings entirely. If the Strait of Hormuz stays closed for longer than six weeks, Stifel warns bills could reach £2,500/year - territory we haven't seen since the worst of the Russia-Ukraine crisis.

Fixed deals are vanishing fast. Energy suppliers have already pulled more than half their fixed tariffs from the market because they can't price the risk. Martin Lewis is telling people to lock in a fix "urgently" while cheap deals still exist. Some of those deals have already disappeared since Monday.

What you can do right now

1

Check if you're on a fixed deal or the price cap. If you're on a standard variable tariff (the default), you're paying the maximum allowed - and that maximum is likely going up in July.

2

Check our tariff tracker. Right now, E.ON Next's tracker tariff is the cheapest on the market. Honestly, there aren't many deals available at the moment - suppliers are pricing up or pulling offers entirely. But it's worth knowing where you stand, and we'll alert you the moment better deals appear.

3

Don't panic - but don't ignore it either. This isn't 2022 yet. Europe has more diverse supply and more LNG terminals now. But if disruption persists, it could get uncomfortable. Being informed is the best defence.

The bigger picture

This is the second time in four years that a foreign conflict has sent UK energy bills spiralling. In 2022, Russia's invasion of Ukraine pushed the price cap above £2,500 and cost the government £78 billion in subsidies. The pattern is the same: the UK remains heavily dependent on gas, and gas is priced on a global market we don't control.
