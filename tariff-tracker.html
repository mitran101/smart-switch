What Are Standing Charges?

Your standing charge is a daily fee you pay regardless of usage. Think of it like a subscription fee for being connected to the energy network. Here's where your money goes:

  • Network Costs (~¬£396/year): Maintaining the physical infrastructure - cables, pipes, substations that bring energy to your home
  • Operating Costs (~¬£270-406/year): Billing systems, customer service, meter reading, account management
  • Policy Costs (~¬£215-236/year): Government schemes like Renewables Obligation, Feed-in Tariffs, ECO (Energy Company Obligation), Warm Home Discount, Nuclear RAB charges
  • Headroom (~¬£18-20/year): A buffer suppliers keep for unexpected costs or market fluctuations

What Are Unit Rates?

Your unit rate is what you pay per kilowatt-hour (kWh) of energy used. Every time you boil the kettle, turn on the heating, or watch TV, you're consuming kWh. Here's the breakdown:

  • Wholesale Energy (~¬£690-720/year): The actual cost of gas and electricity from power stations and the market
  • CfD Payments: Contracts for Difference - subsidies paid to renewable generators to encourage clean energy investment
  • Supplier Margin/EBIT (~¬£42-44/year): The profit suppliers are allowed to make, capped by Ofgem at 1.9% of the total bill
  • Levelisation (~¬£11-13/year): Smoothing costs across seasons (winter prices are higher, so suppliers spread costs across the year)

Why Do Payment Methods Matter?

Direct Debit customers typically save ~¬£90/year compared to Standard Credit. Why?

  • Lower Admin Costs: Automated payments reduce billing and collection costs
  • Reduced Debt Risk: Suppliers face lower bad debt costs, savings passed to customers
  • Cash Flow Predictability: Regular payments help suppliers manage finances better

Ofgem explicitly permits this price difference. Prepayment has historically been more expensive due to meter infrastructure costs and top-up processing, though the gap has narrowed significantly.

High Usage vs Low Usage: What Matters More?

  • Low Usage Households (1-2 people): Standing charge is king! It can be 20-30% of your total bill. Choose tariffs with the lowest standing charge.
  • High Usage Households (4+ people): Unit rate is king! A 1p/kWh difference = ¬£40+/year on typical usage. Choose tariffs with the lowest unit rates.
  • Typical Usage (2-3 people): Both matter equally. Look for tariffs that balance low standing charges with competitive unit rates.
